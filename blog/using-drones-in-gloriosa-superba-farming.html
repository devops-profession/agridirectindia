Using Drones in Gloriosa Superba Farming:

A Smart Approach by Agri Direct India

Published on January 30, 2026 | By AgriDirect India

Gloriosa superba, also known as Kalihari or Flame Lily, is a high-value medicinal plant widely cultivated for its seeds and tubers. It plays a crucial role in pharmaceutical industries due to its colchicine content. However, farming Gloriosa superba requires careful crop management, especially during pest control and nutrient spraying.

Modern technology like agricultural drones is transforming Gloriosa superba farming by making spraying safer, faster, and more efficient. At Agri Direct India, we help farmers adopt drone spraying to improve crop health and maximize profits.

Challenges in Traditional Gloriosa Superba Farming

Farmers cultivating Gloriosa superba often face several challenges:

  • Dense vine growth making manual spraying difficult
  • Risk of plant damage during traditional spraying operations
  • High labor requirements for comprehensive coverage
  • Uneven application of pesticides and fertilizers
  • Health risks due to direct exposure to chemicals
  • High fungal infection rates during the rainy season

This is where drone spraying technology offers a smart solution.

How Drones Help in Gloriosa Superba Farming

1. Precision Spraying Without Crop Damage

Gloriosa superba plants are delicate climbers. Drone spraying ensures uniform chemical application from above, avoiding physical contact with plants and significantly reducing crop damage.

2. Effective Pest and Disease Control

Drones provide fine mist spraying with 200-micron droplets, ensuring better coverage on leaves and stems. This helps control:

  • Leaf-eating insects
  • Fungal infections
  • Pest infestations during critical growth stages

3. Reduced Chemical Usage

Drone spraying uses up to 30‚Äì40% less water and chemicals compared to traditional methods, lowering input costs and preventing overuse of agrochemicals.

4. Safe for Farmers

Gloriosa superba contains toxic compounds. Drone spraying keeps farmers away from direct contact with chemicals, significantly improving workplace safety.

5. Faster Operations During Rainy Season

During the rainy season, fungal infections in Gloriosa superba can escalate rapidly, and timely spraying becomes critical. However, traditional spraying becomes extremely difficult due to:

  • Heavy rainfall and muddy field conditions
  • Waterlogging in low-lying areas
  • Limited field access

Drone spraying allows farmers to spray crops quickly during short weather windows without entering the field. This ensures timely pest and fungal disease control, which is crucial for Gloriosa superba during monsoon months.

Drone Spraying for Fertilizers and Growth Promoters

Beyond pesticides, drones can be effectively used for:

  • Foliar nutrient spraying
  • Micronutrient application
  • Growth promoters during vegetative and flowering stages

This leads to:

  • Healthier, more vigorous vines
  • Better flowering rates
  • Improved seed yield and quality
